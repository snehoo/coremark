The best combination for most Cambridge Lower Secondary students in India: Cambridge past papers for exam-style exposure (free via CAIE); CoreMark for targeted topic practice (Maths, Science, Computing); Khan Academy for free video explanations when a concept is unclear. The problem with resource discovery in India

Cambridge Lower Secondary is a popular curriculum in international and independent schools across India, yet finding good practice material is harder than it should be. Most revision websites are built for IGCSE or A Level, where the student market is far larger globally. Stage 7, 8, and 9 content is either absent, partially covered, or not aligned to Cambridge syllabus codes.

Indian bookstores stock textbooks, not revision materials. Online platforms are either too broad (Khan Academy) or too exam-level-specific (Save My Exams). This guide maps what's actually available in 2026, what each resource is genuinely useful for, and where the gaps are.

1. Cambridge Past Checkpoint Papers

Cambridge Past Checkpoint Papers

Free (limited) / School access

Cambridge releases a limited number of past Checkpoint papers publicly via the Cambridge website and the Cambridge Learner Support portal. Schools that enter students for Checkpoint usually have access to a wider archive through the Cambridge School Support Hub. Each past paper comes with a mark scheme.

Best for: Exam technique practice and exposure to Cambridge question style. Use them once topic knowledge is solid — not as your first revision tool. Gap: Past papers only exist for Stage 9 Checkpoint, not for Stage 7 or Stage 8 internally. Limited supply (3–5 papers typically available per subject).

2. Cambridge University Press Textbooks

Cambridge University Press Lower Secondary Textbooks

Paid (Rs 800–1,500 per book)

Cambridge University Press publishes official textbooks for Mathematics (0862), Science (0893), Computing (0860), and English (0861) at each stage. These are comprehensive: they cover all curriculum content in the correct order, with explanations and practice questions. Available on Amazon India, Flipkart, and school bookshops.

Best for: Initial learning and comprehensive content coverage. Teachers use them in class. Gap: Not structured for revision. The practice questions are not levelled by difficulty, and there are no worked solutions in most editions. A student who is stuck on a specific topic cannot easily find targeted practice within the textbook. Shipping from abroad can add cost and delay.

3. Khan Academy

Khan Academy

Free

Khan Academy is a free online platform with instructional videos and practice questions across Maths and Science. It covers most topics that appear in Cambridge Lower Secondary Maths (particularly Number, Algebra, and Geometry) and has some Science content. The video explanations are genuinely well-produced for many topics.

Best for: Free explanation of a concept that isn't clicking from a textbook or notes. Particularly good for Algebra basics, fractions, and geometry. Gap: Not organised by Cambridge syllabus stage or topic code. A student looking for "Cambridge Stage 8 Pythagoras" cannot go directly to that content — they have to search and map it manually. No Computing content aligned to Cambridge 0860. Question style does not match Cambridge Checkpoint format. No worked solutions in the Cambridge examiner style.

4. Save My Exams

Save My Exams

Free (partial) / Paid subscription

Save My Exams is a UK-based revision platform with strong IGCSE and A Level content. It has some Cambridge Lower Secondary material but it is not its primary focus. What Lower Secondary content exists tends to be at Stage 9 level.

Best for: Stage 9 students who want a preview of IGCSE-level content. Gap: Incomplete for Stage 7 and Stage 8. Question difficulty is often set above the Lower Secondary level. Not useful for Computing (0860). We have a full breakdown in our guide on Save My Exams and Cambridge Lower Secondary.

How to combine resources effectively

No single resource covers everything a Cambridge Lower Secondary student needs. The most effective approach uses each resource for what it does best:

When your child needs to…Use
Learn a topic for the first timeCambridge textbook or school notes
Understand a concept that isn't clickingKhan Academy videos
Practice a specific topic with levelled questions and worked solutionsCoreMark booster for that topic
Build exam technique and time management before CheckpointCambridge past Checkpoint papers
Preview IGCSE-level Stage 9 content (advanced students)Save My Exams (Stage 9 only)
💡
The sequence mattersDon't start with past papers. Past papers are the last step, not the first. Topic practice before exam practice is the most efficient path to a high Checkpoint score.
