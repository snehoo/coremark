Quick answer

Cambridge Lower Secondary Science (0893) covers Biology, Chemistry and Physics in one integrated assessment — gaps in any strand affect the total score without always showing up clearly in a single test mark. This guide compares the resource types available for Stages 7–9 and explains what makes Science revision materials useful for the Checkpoint format.

Science is where many Cambridge Lower Secondary parents hit a wall. Maths gaps are often visible in a test score. Science gaps are trickier — a child can lose marks across all three disciplines (Biology, Chemistry and Physics) in a single paper, and without a science background it's hard to know which resource actually covers the Cambridge 0893 curriculum rather than a generic "secondary science" scope.

Here is a breakdown of what's available, how the resource types differ, and what makes a Cambridge Science resource worth buying.

What Cambridge Lower Secondary Science covers

Cambridge publishes the full Lower Secondary Science (0893) curriculum framework. The three disciplines run concurrently across all three stages:

Biology

  • Stage 7: Cells and their functions, classification of living things, ecosystems and food chains, human reproduction.
  • Stage 8: Cell specialisation, respiration, photosynthesis, inheritance and variation, health and drugs.
  • Stage 9: DNA, genes and genetics, evolution, natural selection, hormones, the immune system.

Chemistry

  • Stage 7: States of matter, mixtures and separation, elements and compounds, the periodic table (introduction).
  • Stage 8: Atoms, molecules and ions, chemical reactions, acids and bases, the reactivity series.
  • Stage 9: Rates of reaction, energy in reactions, the periodic table (Groups and Periods), electrolysis.

Physics

  • Stage 7: Forces and their effects, energy types and transfers, sound and light, the Earth and solar system.
  • Stage 8: Pressure, work and power, electric circuits, magnetism, wave properties.
  • Stage 9: Newton's laws, momentum, electromagnetic induction, radioactivity, space and the universe.

Cambridge Lower Secondary Science — syllabus 0893

A combined Biology, Chemistry and Physics course across Stages 7–9. The Stage 9 Checkpoint (and end-of-stage tests in 7 and 8) assesses all three disciplines together. Cambridge reports a single overall score and separate Biology, Chemistry and Physics strand scores on the 0.0–6.0 scale.

Science has an additional complication compared to maths: the three disciplines inside 0893 require different kinds of knowledge. A student who is strong in Chemistry but weak in Physics needs a different resource from one who is strong in Biology but shaky on both Chemistry and Physics. A single mixed-topic science workbook won't reveal or fix those splits.

What to look for in a Cambridge Science resource

💡
What to check forWritten specifically for Cambridge Lower Secondary Science (0893), not a generic national curriculum. Separates Biology, Chemistry and Physics clearly so you know which discipline each question is testing. Includes key definitions and diagrams, since Cambridge Science questions are heavily definition-based. Includes a parent guide explaining what the correct answer looks like and why a common wrong answer is wrong.

Common mistake

Buying a combined "Science" workbook and assuming it covers the Cambridge 0893 syllabus. Many popular science revision books are written for the UK National Curriculum or GCSE Combined Science, which have a different scope and different assessment vocabulary from Cambridge Lower Secondary Science 0893.

"In Cambridge Science, a wrong definition loses the mark entirely — partial credit doesn't exist. That's different from most other curricula, and most generic resources don't flag it." Snehal Patel

Frequently asked questions

Cambridge Lower Secondary Science (0893) covers three disciplines across Stages 7, 8 and 9: Biology (cells, reproduction, ecosystems, health, genetics), Chemistry (materials, properties, reactions, the periodic table, acids and bases), and Physics (forces, energy, electricity, waves, space). Each stage deepens the concepts from the previous one. The Stage 9 Checkpoint assesses all three disciplines together, so gaps in any one branch affect the overall score.

Cambridge Lower Secondary Science (Stages 7–9) is not the same as GCSE. It sits before GCSE-level study and is designed as a foundation for later IGCSE or GCSE Science. That said, Stage 9 Biology, Chemistry and Physics cover concepts that often surprise parents who remember science from a different curriculum — particularly in Chemistry (periodic table, rates of reaction) and Physics (wave behaviour, electricity calculations).

The most effective approach for non-expert parents is to use resources that include a parent and tutor guide alongside the questions — explaining what the correct answer looks like and what Cambridge examiners specifically reward. This lets you mark your child's work accurately and identify whether errors are in the Biology, Chemistry or Physics strand, rather than just knowing the final answer is wrong.

One at a time, starting with the weakest discipline. The three disciplines in Cambridge 0893 are assessed in one paper but scored separately by strand, so a targeted 'Biology only' session closes the Biology gap more efficiently than a mixed-topic session that moves between all three. Once the weakest strand is improved, mixed-topic timed practice closer to the assessment is useful.
