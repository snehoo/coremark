Stage 7 is the first year of Cambridge Lower Secondary — and also the year where most parents first realise that generic "Year 7 maths" resources from a bookshop don't actually line up with what Cambridge is teaching. The curriculum has its own strand structure and its own assessment vocabulary, and a workbook written for a different curriculum can cover the same number of pages while leaving specific Cambridge strands completely untouched.

Here is a practical breakdown of what's available, how the different resource types compare, and what to check for before spending money on anything.

What Stage 7 Maths actually covers

Cambridge publishes the full curriculum framework for Lower Secondary Mathematics (0862). At Stage 7, the four strands generally cover:

  • Number: integers and place value, prime numbers, factors and multiples, fractions, decimals, percentages, ratio and proportion.
  • Algebra: writing and simplifying expressions, substitution into formulae, sequences (term-to-term and position-to-term rules), coordinates and straight-line graphs.
  • Geometry and Measure: angle properties and triangles, quadrilaterals and polygons, symmetry, transformations (reflection, translation, rotation), perimeter, area, and volume of cuboids.
  • Statistics and Probability: collecting and organising data, mean, median, mode and range, simple probability and sample space.

Why does this list matter? Because it lets you check whether a revision resource actually maps to these Cambridge strands, rather than to a different national curriculum's "Year 7 maths" scheme. The overlap is partial, not complete.

Cambridge Lower Secondary Mathematics — Stage 7

The first of three stages (7, 8, 9) in the Cambridge Lower Secondary programme. Stage 7 lays the algebraic and geometric foundations that deepen at Stage 8 and culminate in the Stage 9 Checkpoint assessment. A weak Stage 7 foundation is the most common root cause of Stage 9 strand-score drops.

Common trap

Picking a generic "Key Stage 3" or "Year 7" workbook because it looks like the right level. These are often written for the English National Curriculum, which does not match Cambridge 0862's strand structure — particularly in Statistics and Algebra, where Cambridge's sequencing diverges noticeably.

Frequently asked questions

Cambridge Lower Secondary Mathematics (0862) at Stage 7 covers four strands: Number (integers, place value, fractions, decimals, percentages, ratio), Algebra (expressions, formulae, sequences, graphs), Geometry and Measure (angles, shapes, transformations, area and perimeter), and Statistics and Probability (data collection, averages, simple probability). These strands form the foundation that builds through Stage 8 and into the Stage 9 Checkpoint assessment.

Stage 7 introduces many algebraic and geometric concepts for the first time, so gaps at Stage 7 tend to compound into Stage 8 and 9 if not addressed early. The content is less demanding than Stage 9, but a weak foundation in Stage 7 Number or Algebra often explains lower strand scores in later years.

Cambridge distributes specimen and past papers for the Checkpoint assessment to registered schools, not to the public directly. Parents looking for Stage 7 practice at home typically use independent resources aligned to the 0862 curriculum strands rather than official past papers.

If there is a specific known gap — for example, fractions or ratio — a single-topic pack closes it faster than a mixed workbook, because every question targets that one strand and the solutions show exactly where marks are typically lost. Mixed workbooks are better suited to general timed-practice runs closer to a test, when no single gap stands out.
