What is Computing at Cambridge Lower Secondary?

Computing boosters are single-topic Cambridge Lower Secondary practice packs covering Computational Thinking and Programming, Managing Data, Networks and Digital Communication, and Computer Systems — each with a cheat sheet, 20 leveled questions and full worked solutions.

  • 💻 Computational Thinking and Programming — algorithms, Python and program design
  • 🗄️ Managing Data — data types, databases and spreadsheets
  • 🌐 Networks and Digital Communication — the internet, networks and online safety
  • 🖥️ Computer Systems — hardware, software and how computers work

How do I use a Computing booster?

  1. Read the cheat sheet first. All the key facts and formulas for the topic, in 5 minutes.
  2. Attempt all 20 questions. Work through Foundation → Standard → Stretch in order — each band builds on the last.
  3. Check with the worked solutions. Read the error flags — they show the mistakes Cambridge examiners see most often.
  4. Use the parent and tutor guide. Score bands and study tips tell you what to focus on next.

Common questions

Stage 7 is roughly Year 7 or Class 7, Stage 8 is Year 8 or Class 8, and Stage 9 is Year 9 or Class 9. If your child's school follows the Cambridge Lower Secondary programme, the stage will match their year group. If you are not sure, start with Stage 8 and see how your child gets on with one pack before buying more.
