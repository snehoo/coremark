Stage 9 Number is where Cambridge Checkpoint separates students — standard form, compound interest, and reverse percentages are introduced here, and they require a different approach than any Number topic covered in Stages 7 or 8.

Why Stage 9 Number is different: Stages 7 and 8 Number is mostly procedural. Stage 9 introduces conceptual traps: standard form for numbers between 0 and 1 (negative exponents), reverse percentage (working backwards from the result), and compound vs. simple interest (which rate to use). These are consistently among the highest-error topics in Checkpoint past papers.

What this booster covers — Stage 9 Number (0862)

Standard form: converting to and from standard form, including numbers between 0 and 1
Standard form calculations: multiplying, dividing, adding and subtracting
Compound interest and repeated percentage change using multipliers
Reverse percentages: finding the original value before a percentage change
Upper and lower bounds: calculating and using bounds in calculations
Estimation and significant figures in the context of bounds
